suffering

An oil project i completed as my undergraduate capstone project in may 2021.

Inspired by both neoclassicism & expressionism, i was drawn to depict these myths for a reason
i could not place. After their completion, it became clear that an inner part of me, one which i could not put into words, had come out in visual form. Ultimately, this project was a realization and acceptance of the injustice of life: a resignation to the forces of nature that make some win and others lose.

orpheus & eurydice

oil on canvas. 16"x20".

Theirs is the archetypal tragedy. The grief of
death, the ardor of the journey down and back,
none of it enough to please the whims of the gods. Each eternally punished, but for what crime?

medusa

oil on canvas. 36"x48".

I didn't know why i painted her until after
i finished. Within her petrifying gaze i can
see the sorrow and pain of an innocent
maiden punished and persecuted for glory.

narcissus

oil on canvas. 18"x24".

I was taught the story of narcissus as a condemnation of narcissism and
self-obsession. But i guess i have always found it difficult to absorb the
lesson. As long as i'm not hurting anyone, can i not embrace
my own cultivated qualities?
